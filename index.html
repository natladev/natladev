Why I build what I build

A while back my tech lead, Chris Ward — an advocate for LGBTQ+ rights and neurodivergent communities — gave a talk about the curb cut effect: the ramps cut into curbs for wheelchairs ended up helping everyone, from strollers to suitcases to delivery carts. It stayed with me. I'm autistic and dyslexic, and I realized I could cut the curbs I already know from the inside — designing from lived experience, for everyone's benefit.

Cut one curb, and everyone rolls through.

That thread runs through everything I make. By day I'm an iOS developer who's shipped apps to millions of users; on the side I'm working toward a Master's in Artificial Intelligence, with my thesis still ahead of me. Both of my apps — MyTinnitusMask and ClearPage — grew straight out of that idea, and both are free, forever.

Projects

MyTinnitusMask

In Development

Built from love. My husband has tinnitus. I couldn't make the ringing stop, so I enrolled in a Master's in AI. The result: a free app that learns which masking sound works for each person — psychoacoustic matching plus Bayesian optimization, entirely on-device. Learn more →

iOS Bayesian Optimization Signal Processing AI Research

ClearPage

Removing barriers, not fixing deficits. Dyslexia is a formatting problem, not a reading problem. Snap a photo or screenshot, and ClearPage re-renders text in your layout — your spacing, your typeface, your colors. Free, offline, on-device OCR in English, Spanish, and German.
